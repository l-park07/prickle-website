Prickle ("we," "us," or "our") is an independent app, built and run by a solo developer — there's no company behind it, just one person who has eczema too. If anything here doesn't sit right, please don't use the app — but we'd rather hear from you first at lgpark@parkfam.com.

1.What information we collect

In shortTo run your account, we collect only your email and password. Everything else you type into Prickle stays on your phone.

Information you give us

When you create an account, we collect:

  • your email address
  • a password (stored securely by Google Firebase Authentication — we never see it in readable form)

That's the whole list for account data. You can use most of Prickle without giving us anything more.

Sensitive (health) information

Prickle is a health app, so the entries you make — severity scores, symptoms, triggers, medications, and skin photos — are health data, which privacy laws treat as a special, protected category. Here's the important part: this data is stored only on your device. We don't receive it, we can't read it, and it isn't sent anywhere — unless you deliberately turn on Cloud Sync (see section 3). We never process your health data to infer anything about you, and we never use it for advertising.

Device permissions

The app may ask permission to use:

  • Your camera / photo library — only when you choose to add a skin photo. Photos are saved inside the app on your device, not to your camera roll.
  • Notifications — only if you turn on reminders. You can turn these off anytime in your device settings.

You control all of these in your device's settings.

Google / Firebase

We use Google Firebase for sign-in (and, if you opt in, for Cloud Sync). Our use of Google APIs follows the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements.

2.What stays on your device

In shortYour logs and photos live in the app's own storage on your phone. Deleting the app removes them.

Prickle is local-first by design. Your daily logs, severity scores, triggers, medications, weekly assessments, and photos are stored in a private database on your device. This means:

  • We have no access to your health entries or photos.
  • They are not transmitted to us or to any third party.
  • If you uninstall Prickle without Cloud Sync on, that data is gone — so if you'd like a backup, that's exactly what Cloud Sync is for.

3.Cloud Sync — optional, off by default

In shortCloud Sync is entirely your choice. It backs up your data so you can restore it on a new phone. You can turn it off, and delete the backup, at any time.

Cloud Sync is an opt-in feature. It is switched off until you choose to enable it in the app's settings. It is not required to use Prickle.

What turning it on does: a copy of your logs and photos is securely stored in your private, per-user space in Google Firebase (Firestore and Cloud Storage), linked to your account. This lets you restore your history if you change phones or reinstall the app.

What we do with it: nothing beyond storing it for you. Your synced data is not read by us for any purpose, not analysed, not shared, and never sold or used commercially. Access is restricted by security rules so that only your account can read or write your data.

Turning it off: you can disable Cloud Sync at any time. You can also request that your cloud backup be deleted — see section 17. Turning off Cloud Sync does not affect the copy already on your device.

Because Cloud Sync involves your health data, we ask for your explicit consent before it's enabled, and you can withdraw that consent whenever you like.

4.How we use your information

In shortWe use your account details to let you sign in and keep your account working — and, if you ask, to help with support. That's it.

We process the little information we hold in order to:

  • Create and manage your account and let you sign in.
  • Respond to you if you contact us for support or feedback.
  • Back up and restore your data, but only if you've turned on Cloud Sync.
  • Send you app updates by email, but only if you opted in.
  • Keep the app secure and meet our legal obligations.

Account messages — like a security or account notice — may be sent when they're needed to keep your account working. These aren't optional, because without them the account can't function.

App update emails are optional. When you create your account, you can choose to hear about new features and improvements by email. This is off unless you opt in, you can change it anytime in your account settings, and every email includes an unsubscribe link. We never share or sell your email, and we don't send anything else this way.

5.Legal bases for processing (EU / UK)

In shortWe only process your information when we have a valid legal reason under the GDPR and UK GDPR.

If you are in the EU, EEA, or UK, the legal bases we rely on are:

  • Consent. For enabling Cloud Sync of your health data (explicit consent), and for sending you optional app-update emails, we rely on your consent. You can withdraw it at any time.
  • Performance of a contract. To provide the account and app features you've asked for (creating and maintaining your account).
  • Legal obligations. Where we must process information to comply with the law.
  • Vital interests. In rare cases, to protect someone's life or safety.

Withdrawing consent. Where we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time — by turning off Cloud Sync in the app, turning off app-update emails in your account settings (or using the unsubscribe link in any email), or by contacting us at lgpark@parkfam.com. Withdrawing consent doesn't affect processing that already lawfully happened before you withdrew.

6.When and with whom we share your information

In shortWe don't sell your data, and we don't share your health data. Google Firebase acts as our infrastructure provider, and that's it.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information, and we never will. We have not shared any personal information for advertising or commercial purposes, ever.

The only third party involved is Google Firebase, which provides our sign-in and (if you opt in) Cloud Sync infrastructure, acting on our behalf as a service provider under contract. If the app were ever transferred to another owner, we would tell you before any information moved, and this notice would continue to apply.

7.International data transfers

In shortYour account data — and, if you opt in, your synced data — is stored on Google's servers, which may be located outside your country, including in the United States.

Google Firebase may store and process data on servers in the United States and other countries. If you are in the EU, EEA, or UK, this means your information may be transferred outside your region. Where that happens, the transfer is covered by the safeguards Google provides for its customers, including the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses and, where applicable, the EU–US Data Privacy Framework. You can read more in Firebase's privacy and security documentation.

8.How long we keep your information

In shortWe keep your account for as long as you have one. Your on-device data is yours to keep or delete.

We keep your account information for as long as your account exists. Your on-device logs and photos remain on your device until you delete them or uninstall the app. If Cloud Sync is on, your backup is kept until you turn Cloud Sync off and/or request deletion. When we no longer need information, we delete or anonymise it.

9.How we keep your information safe

In shortWe use reasonable technical and organisational measures — but no system is ever 100% secure.

Account credentials are handled by Google Firebase Authentication. Synced data (if enabled) is protected by encryption in transit and at rest and by per-user security rules, so only your account can access your data. Keeping most data on your own device keeps its exposure low. That said, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, so we can't guarantee absolute security. Please use the app within a secure environment.

10.Children

In shortPrickle is not intended for anyone under 18.

We don't knowingly collect information from children under 18 (or the equivalent age in your jurisdiction). By using Prickle, you confirm you're at least 18, or that you're the parent or guardian consenting on a minor's behalf. If we learn we've collected data from someone under 18, we'll delete it and deactivate the account. If you believe a child has given us information, contact us at lgpark@parkfam.com.

11.Your privacy rights

In shortYou can access, correct, export, delete, or restrict your data, and withdraw consent, at any time.

Depending on where you live (including the EEA, UK, Switzerland, Canada, and many US states), you may have the right to:

  • access a copy of your personal information;
  • correct information that's wrong;
  • delete your information;
  • export your information (data portability);
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • withdraw consent (for example, by turning off Cloud Sync);
  • not be subject to automated decision-making — and note that Prickle makes no automated decisions about you.

To exercise any of these, contact us at lgpark@parkfam.com. Much of it you can also do yourself inside the app: edit your entries, delete your account, or toggle Cloud Sync.

If you're in the UK and unhappy with how we've handled your data, you can complain to us, and then to the Information Commissioner's Office (helpline 0303 123 1113). If you're in the EEA, you can complain to your local data protection authority. In Switzerland, contact the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner.

12.Do-Not-Track

Most browsers offer a "Do-Not-Track" (DNT) signal. Because there's no finalised industry standard for these signals, we don't currently respond to them. Prickle doesn't track your browsing across sites in any case.
